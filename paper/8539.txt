Lawrence J. Christiano and Massimo Rostagno
NBER Working Paper No. 8539
October 2001
JEL No. E52, E31, E63

ABSTRACT
Using a series of examples, we review the various ways in which a monetary policy characterized
by the Taylor rule can inject volatility into the economy. In the examples, a particular modification to the
Taylor rule can reduce or even entirely eliminate the problems. Under the modified policy, the central
bank monitors the money growth rate and commits to abandoning the Taylor rule in favor of a money
growth rule in case money growth passes outside a particular monitoring range.
