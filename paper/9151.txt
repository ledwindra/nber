                                             ABSTRACT

       Will an industry with no antitrust policy converge to monopoly, competition, or somewhere in
between? We analyze this question using a dynamic dominant firm model with rational agents,
endogenous mergers, and constant returns to scale production. We find that perfect competition and
monopoly are always steady states of this model, and that there may be other steady states with a
dominant firm and a fringe co-existing. Mergers are likely only when supply is inelastic or demand is
elastic, suggesting that the ability of a dominant firm to raise price, through monopolization is limited.
Additionally, as the discount factor increases, it becomes harder to monopolize the industry, because the
dominant firm cannot commit to not raising prices in the future.
