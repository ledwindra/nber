ABSTRACT
I review The End of Alchemy by Mervyn King, published by W.W. Norton and Company in
2016. I discuss King's proposed regulatory reform, the ‘Pawn Broker for All Seasons’ (PFAS)
and I compare it to an alternative solution developed in my own work. I argue that unregulated
trade in the financial markets will not, in general, lead to Pareto optimal allocations. As a
consequence, solutions like the PFAS that correct problems with existing institutions are likely to
be circumvented by the development of new ones.
